TRITON / TRISIS Safety System Compromise

Incident Summary: Unprecedented malware designed to manipulate industrial safety systems, representing an escalation from IT disruption to physical kinetic threat.

The Attack Vector

Attackers gained an initial foothold on the IT network before moving laterally into the OT environment. They compromised an engineering workstation that was used to program the plant's Triconex Safety Instrumented Systems (SIS).

Purdue Model Failure

The core architectural failure was the lack of strict segmentation between Level 2 (Supervisory Control) and Level 1 (Basic Control/Safety). The SIS network should operate entirely autonomously from the primary DCS to prevent common-mode failures.

> SCANNING TRICONEX SIS NODES...
> VULNERABILITY DETECTED: UNRESTRICTED ENGINEERING ACCESS
> PAYLOAD INJECTED TO FIRMWARE

The ThreatForge Remediation

For our clients, mitigating this threat involves implementing hardware-enforced unidirectional gateways (data diodes) for SIS monitoring and enforcing physical key switches for any logic updates to Level 1 safety controllers.

Ensure your safety systems are truly isolated.
