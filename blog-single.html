Why Flat OT Networks Are a Ticking Time Bomb

Published: May 2025 | By: ThreatForge Consultant

One of the most common architectural flaws we encounter during ICS Risk Assessments is the dreaded "flat" OT network. In these environments, every device from a Level 3 Historian down to a Level 1 PLC resides on the same subnet, fully routable and entirely unsegmented.

The Illusion of the Air Gap

Many plant managers operate under the assumption that their network is "air-gapped" because it lacks a direct internet connection. However, the reality is that OT networks are heavily interconnected with IT systems for data collection, vendor remote support, and business analytics.

Implementing Zones and Conduits

The solution, prescribed by IEC 62443, is to implement a Zone and Conduit model. This involves grouping assets with similar security requirements into distinct zones and strictly controlling the communication conduits between them. Crucially, a demilitarized zone (IDMZ) must sit between the enterprise network (Level 4) and the manufacturing operations (Level 3).
